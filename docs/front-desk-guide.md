# V-Health Recovery Coach — Front Desk Guide

**For:** Clinic reception and front desk staff
**Last updated:** 2026-04-03

---

## What is V-Health Recovery Coach?

V-Health Recovery Coach is a free text message and online chat service for patients recovering from injuries. After their appointment, patients can text a special phone number or visit a website to check in on their recovery. An AI assistant responds, tracks their pain levels and exercises, and sends them a weekly progress summary.

It is **not a replacement for treatment** — it is a support tool that helps patients stay on track between appointments and helps the clinic understand how patients are doing at home.

---

## How to Introduce It to Patients

### When to bring it up

- At check-out after an initial assessment or follow-up appointment
- When the practitioner has indicated the patient is enrolled or should be enrolled

### Suggested script (English)

> "Before you go — your physiotherapist has enrolled you in our recovery coaching service. It's completely free. You'll get a text message asking you to get started. You just reply to the texts to check in on how you're feeling, and you'll get a weekly summary of your progress. It only takes a minute or two. If you ever want to stop, just text STOP and that's it."

### Suggested script (Chinese — Mandarin)

> "在您离开之前——您的物理治疗师已经为您注册了我们的康复指导服务。这是完全免费的。您很快会收到一条短信，引导您开始。您只需回复短信，告诉我们您的恢复情况，每周还会收到一份进度总结。只需要一两分钟。如果您想停止，只需回复STOP即可。"

### Tips for a smooth introduction

- Keep it brief — patients are often tired after their appointment.
- Emphasize it is **free** and **optional**.
- If the patient looks uncertain, reassure them: "You can always opt out by replying STOP — no obligation."
- Do not promise specific features or make medical claims about the service.

## How to Sign Up a Patient

Reception staff sign up patients using the admin dashboard.

### Steps

1. Open **https://vhealth.ai/dashboard** on your computer and log in.
2. Click **Add Patient**.
3. Enter the patient's **mobile phone number** — include the area code (e.g., `604-123-4567` or `+16041234567`).
4. Enter the patient's **name** (optional — the patient can provide it themselves during onboarding).
5. Enter the **practitioner's name** (optional — for reference).
6. Click **Save**.

That is it. The patient will receive a welcome text message within a few seconds.

### What the patient sees

The patient receives this text:

> "Welcome to V-Health Recovery Coach! By continuing, you agree to our privacy policy: vhealth.ai/privacy. Reply YES to continue or STOP to opt out."

They then answer a few quick questions — their name, their condition, and their language preference. After that, they are all set up.

### If you make a mistake (wrong number, etc.)

Let the clinic manager know. They can correct the patient record in the dashboard.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### "What if the patient doesn't want to participate?"

That is completely fine. The service is optional. Just let them know they can opt out at any time by texting **STOP** to the V-Health number. If they do not want to sign up at all, simply do not add them to the system.

Never pressure a patient to participate. If they express hesitation, say: "No worries — it's totally optional. You can always ask your physiotherapist about it at your next appointment."

### "What data do you collect?"

The app collects:
- The patient's mobile phone number
- Their name and the type of injury or condition they described
- Their text messages and chat conversations with the AI
- Health metrics they report (pain levels, exercise completion, etc.)

All data is stored securely on encrypted servers in Canada/USA. Only the clinic admin and the patient can see their data. Full details are in the privacy policy at **vhealth.ai/privacy**.

### "Is this replacing their treatment?"

No. V-Health Recovery Coach supports recovery between appointments — it does not replace physiotherapy treatment, medical advice, or clinical judgment. The AI is not a doctor or a physiotherapist. It cannot diagnose conditions, prescribe exercises, or make clinical recommendations. It encourages patients, tracks their self-reported progress, and flags concerns to the clinic.

If a patient asks health-related questions that go beyond general encouragement, the AI will direct them back to their physiotherapist.

### "How much does it cost the patient?"

**Nothing.** The service is completely free for patients. There are no fees, subscriptions, or in-app purchases.

Patients use their regular text messaging. If they have a standard Canadian mobile plan with unlimited texting, there is no additional cost. If they have a pay-per-text plan, standard SMS rates from their carrier may apply.

### "What if they want to stop?"

The patient can stop at any time in two ways:
1. **Text STOP** to the V-Health SMS number. They will immediately stop receiving all messages.
2. **Contact the clinic** and we can deactivate their account in the dashboard.

If a patient texts STOP and later wants to re-join, they can text **START** to the same number.

### "What if the patient doesn't speak English or French?"

The app supports **English and Chinese (Mandarin)**. During setup, the patient selects their preferred language by replying `1` for English or `2` for Chinese. The AI will respond in whichever language the patient uses.

For patients who speak other languages, the service is not currently available in their language. Let them know and document that in their clinic file.

### "What if the patient doesn't have a smartphone?"

They only need a basic mobile phone that can receive and send text messages — no smartphone or internet required. The SMS service works on any mobile plan.

The web chat feature (`vhealth.ai/chat`) does require a smartphone or computer with internet access, but patients can use SMS only and still get full value from the service.

### "What if a patient reports an emergency through the app?"

The system automatically detects messages that may indicate an emergency (extreme pain, distress, or crisis language). When this happens:

1. The patient immediately receives a message with V-Health's contact number and instructions to call 911 if needed.
2. The clinic admin receives an email alert.

Reception staff do not need to do anything special. If you receive a call from a patient who seems to be in distress, follow the clinic's normal emergency protocol.

## Printed Handout Card — Design Specification

The following spec is for a business-card-sized handout (3.5" x 2") that can be printed in bulk and given to patients at check-out.

### Front of card

**Layout:** Two columns

**Left column (60% width):**
- Headline (large, bold): "V-Health Recovery Coach"
- Subhead (small): "Free SMS recovery support between your appointments"
- Body (tiny): "Text updates on your pain and exercises. Get a weekly progress report. Works on any phone."
- Clinic phone number (prominent): e.g., 604-XXX-XXXX
- Opt-out note (very small): "Text STOP to unsubscribe at any time"

**Right column (40% width):**
- QR code linking to **https://vhealth.ai**
- Label below QR code: "Scan to chat online"

**Color scheme:** Use V-Health brand colors (teal/navy). Keep background white for print clarity.

### Back of card

**Layout:** Single column, centered

**Content:**
- "How it works" heading
- Three numbered steps:
  1. "You'll get a welcome text — reply YES to start"
  2. "Text us how you're feeling, anytime"
  3. "Get a weekly progress summary"
- Reassurance line: "Private, secure, and always free"
- Website: vhealth.ai

### Print notes

- Minimum font size: 7pt for body text, 9pt for headings
- QR code: minimum 0.8" x 0.8" for reliable scanning
- Use a matte finish for better readability
- Print double-sided on 14pt card stock
- Keep a stack at the front desk and hand one to each enrolled patient
