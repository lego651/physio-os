# Market Research — PhysioOS

> Last updated: 2026-04-01

---

## Market Overview

The digital physiotherapy and recovery coaching market is growing rapidly, driven by:
- Rising MSK (musculoskeletal) conditions — #1 cause of disability globally (WHO)
- Post-COVID acceleration of virtual health services
- Employer-driven demand to reduce healthcare costs
- Growing consumer comfort with AI health tools

**Market size:** Digital therapeutics for MSK estimated at $4.2B by 2027 (Grand View Research).

---

## Competitive Landscape

### Enterprise Players (B2B-to-Employer)

| Company | What They Do | Pricing | Differentiator | Weakness |
|---------|-------------|---------|----------------|----------|
| **Hinge Health** | Digital MSK clinic — exercise therapy, health coaching, wearable sensors, 1:1 PT access | B2B employer-sponsored; valued $6B+ | Combines wearable sensors with human coaching at scale | Enterprise-only; no self-pay; expensive per-member |
| **Sword Health** | AI-powered digital PT with motion-tracking sensors and virtual PT sessions | B2B employer/health plan | FDA-listed digital therapeutics; AI "Phoenix" therapist | Hardware dependency (sensor kit); enterprise-only |
| **Kaia Health** | AI-powered digital therapy for MSK pain; phone camera for real-time exercise form correction | B2B employer/insurer | Computer vision motion tracking via phone camera | No direct-to-consumer; limited to partner networks |

### Clinician Tools (B2B-to-Clinic)

| Company | What They Do | Pricing | Differentiator | Weakness |
|---------|-------------|---------|----------------|----------|
| **Physitrack** | SaaS for clinicians — prescribe home exercises, track adherence, collect outcomes | $30-80/month per practitioner | 4,000+ exercise library; telehealth built in | Not patient-facing standalone; **no AI coaching** |
| **PhysiApp** | Patient companion app to Physitrack | Free (bundled with Physitrack) | Clean patient UX tied to clinician oversight | No autonomous AI; entirely dependent on clinician input |
| **Jane App** | Clinic management — booking, charting, billing, insurance | $54-149/month per practitioner | Full practice management suite; widely used in Canada | **No patient engagement outside appointments; no AI** |

### Emerging / Niche

| Company | What They Do | Pricing | Differentiator | Weakness |
|---------|-------------|---------|----------------|----------|
| **Kemtai** | Computer vision exercise platform — real-time form feedback via webcam | B2B and B2C tiers | Browser-based motion tracking (no hardware) | Less clinical validation; small library |
| **Limber Health** | AI-driven exercise prescription for post-surgical/chronic pain | B2B health systems | Automated evidence-based program generation | Narrow clinical focus; low consumer awareness |

## The Gap We Fill

**No major player offers a conversational AI recovery companion.**

Every competitor focuses on one of:
1. Exercise libraries + video demos (Physitrack, PhysiApp)
2. Motion tracking + sensors (Hinge, Sword, Kaia)
3. Practice management + booking (Jane App)

**Nobody does:**
- Daily conversational check-ins via SMS/web chat
- Quantified recovery metrics (pain scores, discomfort levels, sitting tolerance) tracked over time through natural conversation
- AI-generated pattern detection ("your discomfort spikes after skipping stretches")
- Proactive weekly progress reports with data visualization
- Bilingual (EN/CN) conversational support
- White-label deployment for local clinics as a patient engagement tool

## Our Positioning

### For local clinics (V-Health model):

> "Give your patients a 24/7 AI recovery companion — they stay engaged between appointments, you see their progress before they walk in."

**We are NOT:**
- A practice management replacement (Jane App keeps its role)
- A telehealth platform (we don't do video consultations)
- A diagnosis tool (we track, we don't diagnose)

**We ARE:**
- The engagement layer between appointments
- A data collection system that makes patient progress measurable
- A white-label tool that makes clinics stand out from competitors

### Why clinics would adopt:
1. **Patient retention** — patients who track recovery stay engaged and rebook
2. **Competitive differentiation** — "we offer AI-powered recovery coaching" is a marketing advantage
3. **Better appointments** — practitioner sees recovery data before the session
4. **Low effort** — clinic only needs to onboard patients; AI does the rest

### Why patients would use it:
1. **Instant feedback** — don't wait for next appointment to ask questions
2. **Effortless logging** — just text how you feel; AI structures it
3. **Measurable progress** — see your pain scores trending down over weeks
4. **Always available** — SMS or web, any time

## Risks and Considerations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Patients stop logging after 2 weeks | Active nudges, practitioner follow-up alerts, keep logging to <30 seconds |
| Clinics don't see value | Pre-appointment summaries prove immediate value; free pilot period |
| AI gives incorrect advice | Strict guardrails; always defer to practitioner; no diagnosis/prescription |
| Regulatory concerns (PHI data) | PIPEDA compliance; Canadian data residency; consent workflows |
| Competition from big players moving downstream | Speed to market; local clinic relationships; bilingual advantage in Canadian market |
